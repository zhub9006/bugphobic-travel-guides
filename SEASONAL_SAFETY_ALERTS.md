## ⚠️ Critical Seasonal Alerts

### 🐜 MOSQUITTO PEAK SEASONS (Avoid or Take Precautions)
- **June–August:** Mongolian steppe, Gobi riverbanks, eastern forests — peak mosquito and tick season
- **May–July:** Khentii Mountains (Mongolia) — ticks in grassy meadows
- **January–March (Southern):** Hawaii (wet windward sides) — increased mosquito activity
- **May–October (Tropical):** New Caledonia wet season — sandflies increase
- **September–October:** Montserrat lowlands — wet season mosquito return

### 🌸 ALLERGEN + BUG OVERLAP SEASONS
- **April–May (Northern):** Spring bloom = more flies near flowers; choose urban destinations
- **September–October (Northern):** Falling leaves decompose = more gnats near forests; stick to dry areas
- **November (Tropical):** Rainy season in some regions = mosquito breeding begins

## 🌡️ Climate-Based Bug Predictions

### El Niño / La Niña Impact on Bug Activity
- **El Niño years:** Typically wetter in normally dry areas (Atacama, Chile) → slightly more insect potential; drier in SE Asia → fewer bugs in tropical destinations
- **La Niña years:** Typically drier in normal wet areas → can reduce bug populations; wetter in usually dry areas → slight increase in insect activity

### General Climate Notes
- **Warmer global temperatures** are gradually expanding mosquito season in some regions — always check current CDC/Zika alerts before traveling
- **Drier-than-average years** in desert destinations (Atacama, Sedona, Gobi) = even better bug-free conditions
- **Windier seasons** (Curaçao, Patagonia) = naturally fewer flying insects
