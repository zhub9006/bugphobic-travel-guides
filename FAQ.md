# ❓ Frequently Asked Questions — Bug-Free Travel

## General Questions

### Are there really ANY completely bug-free destinations?
**A:** Yes! **Antarctica, Iceland, and Svalbard** are confirmed zero-bug. For almost-zero, the Atacama Desert, Gobi Desert, and high-altitude/arid destinations are excellent choices.

### Do I still need bug spray if I visit a "bug-free" destination?
**A:** For true zero-bug destinations (Antarctica, Iceland, Svalbard), no. But for "very low" destinations, carry a small bottle of DEET as a backup.

### What causes mosquitoes to not exist in some places?
- **Extreme cold** (Antarctica, Svalbard) — insects can't survive
- **No standing water** (Atacama, Gobi, Sedona) — mosquitoes can't breed
- **Constant wind** (Patagonia, Curaçao, Maldives) — insects can't fly
- **Altitude** (Puebla at 2,100m) — too cool for tropical insects

### I have a severe insect phobia. Where should I go first?
**A:** Start with **Iceland** — accessible (6 hr from NYC), incredible sights (Northern Lights, geysers, waterfalls), and 100% bug-free. Then consider **Antarctica** for the ultimate zero-bug experience.

## Destination-Specific Q&A

### The Maldives: Are the Maldives really bug-free?
A: It depends where you stay! **Private resort islands** are extremely well-managed. **Local islands and budget guesthouses** may have more insects. Always choose a resort island!

### Hawaii: Is Hawaii safe for bug-phobic travelers?
A: Yes, BUT — **stick to the leeward (dry) side** (Kona on Big Island, Waikiki on Oahu). The windward (wet) sides (Hilo, Hanalei) have significantly more mosquitoes.

### Curaçao: Is Curaçao buggy?
A: Not at the resort level! Much **drier and windier** than other Caribbean islands. Main resort areas (Willemstad, Westpunt) are virtually bug-free.

### Patagonia: Are there mosquitoes in Patagonia?
A: Extremely rare. Strong winds (60+ km/h) make most of Patagonia inhospitable to insects. The only exceptions are **sheltered forest valleys** in Torres del Paine during Jan–Feb. Stay on open trails!

### New Caledonia: Malaria/dengue?
A: No! **No malaria, no dengue, no Zika** — strict French biosecurity.
